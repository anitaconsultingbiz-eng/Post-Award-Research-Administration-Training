Event 11

Subrecipient Monitoring Problem

Event 10 asked whether CHAN should be treated as a subrecipient. Event 11 asks what happens after the subaward is active and the university must monitor both the money and the work.

Year 2 Event 11 Subrecipient Monitoring

Story Background

Issuing the subaward was only the beginning

Community Health Access Network, or CHAN, was added to the project during Event 10 and classified as a subrecipient. Karen Mitchell and her team now participate in community outreach, recruitment support, educational programming, and community engagement.

For several months, the partnership has operated successfully. Quarterly invoices have been submitted and reimbursed, and the project continues to expand its reach into the community.

David Patel and Marcus Rivera each have a role in reviewing CHAN’s invoice package. David reviews the financial side of the invoice, including the amount requested, cost detail, budget alignment, and payment process. Marcus gathers the monitoring information needed to support the invoice, including whether required progress reports, deliverables, and performance documentation are included in the project file.

During routine invoice review, David flags CHAN’s latest invoice package as incomplete. The invoice includes the amount requested for payment, but it does not include enough performance documentation to show what work was completed during the billing period.

David sends the issue to Marcus Rivera because the missing support is not only a payment question. It is also a subrecipient monitoring issue. Marcus must determine what documentation is missing, follow up with CHAN, document the monitoring action, and update Research Finance before the invoice can move forward.

The invoice may look reasonable, but financial information alone is not enough. Subrecipient monitoring includes both financial review and performance review.

Trigger Issue

David flags an incomplete invoice package

From: David Patel, Research Finance

To: Marcus Rivera, Research Administrator

Subject: CHAN Invoice Review

Hi Marcus,

I am reviewing the latest invoice package from Community Health Access Network for the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative.

The invoice appears to be complete from a cost perspective, and the charges are consistent with the subaward budget. However, I do not see the required quarterly progress report attached to the invoice package.

Could you review the monitoring requirement and follow up on the missing performance documentation before Research Finance moves this invoice forward?

Thank you,
David Patel
Research Finance

Monitoring Concept

Subrecipient monitoring is more than invoice review

Once a subaward is active, the university has ongoing oversight responsibilities. David reviews the invoice from a financial perspective. Marcus reviews whether the required monitoring documentation is present and whether CHAN’s reported work supports the invoice package.

Financial Activity

Are the charges consistent with the approved subaward budget and reimbursement requirements?

Performance Progress

Is CHAN completing outreach, recruitment, and engagement work as expected?

Reporting Compliance

Are required progress reports, deliverables, and supporting records submitted on time?

Risk Indicators

Missing reports, late reports, unsupported costs, or poor communication may signal increased monitoring risk.

Documentation Review

Records should verify that project activities occurred and that reimbursement requests are appropriate.

Subrecipient Performance

The institution must monitor whether the subrecipient is meeting project expectations throughout the subaward period.

Marcus’s Review

Questions before the invoice moves forward

Marcus is not acting alone as the final payment authority. His role is to review the monitoring requirement, follow up on the missing performance documentation, and update David once the invoice package is complete from the monitoring side.

Does the invoice appear reasonable?

Marcus reviews the amount, budget category, period covered, and whether charges are consistent with the subaward.

What documentation is required?

Marcus checks the subaward terms and institutional monitoring procedures to confirm what must be included.

What does the invoice not show?

An invoice shows costs. It may not show whether community outreach, recruitment, or educational programming occurred.

Is this a risk indicator?

Missing performance documentation may be an administrative oversight, but it still requires follow-up.

Should the invoice move forward?

Marcus recommends that the invoice package remain on hold for monitoring review until the required progress report is received.

How should Marcus communicate?

Monitoring should be professional and collaborative. The goal is to correct the documentation gap, not assume misconduct.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do before the invoice moves forward?

David has flagged CHAN’s invoice package as financially consistent but incomplete because the required quarterly progress report is missing. What should Marcus do?

Resolution

Marcus follows up with Karen and updates David

David keeps the invoice from moving forward while the package is incomplete. Marcus follows up with Karen to obtain the missing performance documentation, documents the monitoring action, and updates David after the required report is received.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“Monitoring only means reviewing invoices.”

Monitoring includes financial review, performance review, deliverables, reporting, and compliance activities.

“Missing documentation automatically means misconduct.”

Missing documentation may result from administrative error and should be investigated before conclusions are reached.

“Monitoring happens only when problems arise.”

Monitoring is an ongoing responsibility throughout the life of the subaward.

Key Takeaway

Administrative decisions create future responsibilities

Event 10 determined the relationship. Event 11 shows that once a subrecipient relationship exists, the institution must monitor both the money and the work.

Financial information alone is not sufficient

An invoice can look correct while still lacking the performance documentation needed to support reimbursement.

Documentation supports accountability

Progress reports help connect costs to project activity and deliverables.

Monitoring protects the partnership

Professional follow-up helps both the university and the subrecipient meet their responsibilities.
