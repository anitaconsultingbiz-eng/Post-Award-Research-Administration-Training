The Final Year Begins

Finishing the work is not the same as closing the award

By Year 3, Dr. Brown’s project is established. The team has recruited participants, worked with community partners, managed payroll and effort issues, monitored CHAN as a subrecipient, and corrected financial activity when needed.

But the final year changes the pressure. Remaining balances may create temptation to expand activity. Reporting deadlines become more important. Audit documentation must be ready. Personnel changes can affect continuity. Subrecipient and financial records must be cleaned up before final reporting.

Marcus is not closing the award alone. He coordinates with Dr. Brown, David, CHAN, project staff, and the appropriate institutional offices so the project-side record supports final reporting and closeout.

Year 3 asks one main question: Can the project finish responsibly and close the award cleanly?

Year 3 Story Sequence

How the closeout year unfolds

The Year 3 events follow the project as it approaches its end date. Each issue shows how unresolved details can become closeout risk if they are not reviewed, documented, and coordinated early.

13

Carryforward Surprise

A remaining balance appears available, but Marcus must confirm whether the funds can carry forward, whether sponsor rules allow the use, and whether the balance reflects actual unobligated funds after pending items are considered.

14

Scope Creep

The team wants to expand outreach into new communities. Marcus identifies that the work may go beyond the approved scope, add costs, affect compliance, and require consultation with the sponsored programs or sponsor office before anything moves forward.

15

Reporting Deadline Missed

Marcus discovers that a required sponsor report is overdue. He coordinates with Dr. Brown, David, and project staff to confirm status, complete the report through the proper process, document the issue, and strengthen reporting controls.

16

Audit Request

The sponsor requests documentation for selected charges. Marcus helps assemble support packages that connect receipts, approvals, activity records, monitoring files, and cost transfer documentation into a clear project record.

17

Key Personnel Departure

A project staff change creates continuity risk. Marcus helps review access, records, responsibilities, sponsor expectations, and transition planning so work and documentation are not lost.

18

No-Cost Extension Request

Dr. Brown needs more time to complete approved work. Marcus reviews the remaining activities, available funds, CHAN status, sponsor requirements, and routing path before helping prepare the request for review.

19

Final Reconciliation

David begins final financial review. Marcus helps confirm project-side status for open obligations, final payroll, CHAN’s final invoice, incentive reconciliation, cost corrections, and documentation needed before final reporting.

20

Award Closeout

The project reaches its final administrative stage. Marcus helps connect final reports, financial reconciliation, subrecipient closeout, unresolved issues, and retained records into a complete closeout picture.

Post-Award Lens

What learners should notice in Year 3

Year 3 is not just about finishing tasks. It is about making sure the project can support what happened across the entire award period. Every prior decision becomes part of the closeout record: approvals, costs, corrections, reports, monitoring actions, partner activity, and documentation.

Marcus must be especially careful not to overstep. Some decisions require Research Finance, sponsored programs, compliance offices, the sponsor, the PI, or subrecipient input. His role is to gather facts, identify risk, coordinate review, document the record, and help the correct process move forward.

By the end of Year 3, learners should understand that closeout is not an event. It is the result of award management decisions made throughout the full project lifecycle.
