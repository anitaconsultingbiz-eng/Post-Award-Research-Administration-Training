Year 1 Review

These were never separate problems

Over the course of Year 1, Dr. Aneka Brown’s project experienced several situations that are common in sponsored research. Some involved compliance. Others involved finances. Some required difficult judgment calls. Others required careful documentation and monitoring.

At first glance, these situations may appear unrelated: a delayed approval, a vacant position, a conference invitation, a restaurant charge, an equipment purchase, and a discrepancy in participant incentive records.

But each event revealed something important about the role of research administration. Together, they tell a larger story about stewardship, accountability, and decision-making.

This capstone is not simply about remembering what happened. It is about understanding why these situations mattered and how Marcus approached them.

Year 1 Story Arc

What happened across the first year

Year 1 was not a series of unrelated problems. Each event revealed a different kind of post-award responsibility: confirming approvals, reading financial signals, reviewing costs, documenting decisions, and strengthening controls before the project moved deeper into active work.

Administrator Toolkit

The frameworks Marcus used throughout Year 1

Marcus did not approach each event as a one-off problem. He used repeatable questions and review frameworks that helped him gather information, understand requirements, document decisions, and support the project.

Compliance Review

Used when deciding whether project activity can begin.

  • Have approvals been obtained?
  • Are sponsor or institutional restrictions in place?
  • Can the activity begin now?

Financial Monitoring

Used when spending does not match expectations.

  • Does spending match project activity?
  • Are there significant variances?
  • Is project implementation behind schedule?

Expense Review

Used before deciding whether a cost belongs on the award.

  • Is it allowable?
  • Is it allocable?
  • Is it reasonable?
  • Is it documented?

Documentation Review

Used when records need to support a decision.

  • Does the documentation explain what happened?
  • Does it explain why it happened?
  • Would someone unfamiliar with the project understand it?

Rebudgeting Review

Used when project needs change from the original budget.

  • Why is the change needed?
  • Which budget categories are affected?
  • Are sponsor approvals required?

Internal Control Review

Used when records, transactions, or processes need verification.

  • Can transactions be verified?
  • Are reconciliations performed?
  • Are discrepancies investigated?

Marcus’s repeated pattern: stop, gather information, understand the requirements, document the decision, and support the project.

Completion Criteria

What successful Year 1 completion means

Completing Year 1 means the learner has worked through the foundational post-award concepts and can apply administrative thinking to common project management situations.

Complete the curriculum

Review the pre-award story, Events 01–07, and the Year 1 capstone.

Review the materials

Use the Year 1 review, timeline, key concepts guide, and administrator toolkit to reinforce the learning.

Complete the practice scenarios

Work through the capstone practice scenarios and think through the documents, questions, risks, and next steps.

Demonstrate administrative thinking

Show that you can gather information, identify risks, evaluate expenditures, interpret financial information, recognize documentation requirements, and apply internal control concepts.

Completion is self-paced. Learners are ready to move into Year 2 when they can explain the issue, identify the records to review, ask the right follow-up questions, and document a reasonable administrative decision.

Year 1 Mindset

The pattern Marcus used all year

Understand the requirements

Marcus checks sponsor terms, institutional policies, federal rules, IRB status, and approved budgets.

Review the facts

Marcus uses emails, financial reports, receipts, logs, cost estimates, and project records to understand what happened.

Ask questions

Marcus requests missing information instead of guessing or assuming.

Document the decision

Marcus records the basis for decisions so the project can defend them later.

Support the project

Marcus helps Dr. Brown and the project team keep moving responsibly.

Protect the institution

Marcus reduces compliance, financial, documentation, and audit risk.

This is the mindset that separates administrative processing from research administration.

Transition to Year 2

From startup to active award management

By the end of Year 1, the project is no longer in startup mode. Participant recruitment is underway, data collection is active, expenditures are increasing, and operational processes have been established.

Payroll and effort

Year 2 will focus more closely on whether payroll charges are accurate and whether effort matches salary support.

Community partnerships

As participation expands, partnership documentation and monitoring become more important.

Subrecipient monitoring

If project funds are provided to another organization, oversight responsibilities expand.

Cost transfers

As activity increases, mistakes may need correction, and corrections must be documented.

Reporting responsibilities

Sponsors expect accurate reports, tracked deadlines, and documented performance.

More complex judgment

Year 2 builds on Year 1, but the situations become less obvious and the risks become greater.

Year 1 asked: “Can we start?” Year 2 increasingly asks: “Are we managing the project appropriately?”
