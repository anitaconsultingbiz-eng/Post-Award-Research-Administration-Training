Year 3 Review

Closeout is where the full award record is tested

By the end of Year 3, the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative has reached the final stage of award management. The research activities are ending, final reports are being prepared, and the financial record must be reconciled.

But closeout does not only look at what happened in the final month. It reflects the entire life of the award. Every approval, budget decision, payroll correction, effort review, subrecipient monitoring action, cost transfer, report, and audit response becomes part of the record.

This capstone asks you to think like Marcus at the end of the award: what is complete, what is unresolved, what needs support, and what could create risk after closeout?

Capstone Activity

Prepare a final closeout readiness review

In this capstone, you step into Marcus’s role and prepare a final closeout readiness review. The goal is not to memorize the events. The goal is to explain whether the award record is complete, supported, reconciled, and ready for final submission.

1. Review remaining funds

Are funds obligated, carried forward appropriately, spent correctly, or returned according to sponsor requirements?

2. Review scope and approvals

Did the project stay within approved scope, or were changes reviewed and approved?

3. Review reporting status

Are progress, financial, subrecipient, and final reports complete and documented?

4. Review documentation

Can selected costs and major decisions be supported by the project file?

5. Review unresolved obligations

Are final invoices, payroll, purchase orders, incentives, subrecipient records, and corrections resolved?

6. Review closeout readiness

Is the project ready for final reports, record retention, and post-closeout review?

Administrator Toolkit

The Year 3 closeout review frameworks

Marcus uses these frameworks to move from event-by-event review to a complete closeout readiness assessment.

Carryforward Review

  • Is the balance accurate?
  • Are all obligations identified?
  • Is carryforward automatic or restricted?
  • Is sponsor approval required?

Scope Change Review

  • Does the activity match approved objectives?
  • Does it change sites, population, or methods?
  • Does it affect IRB or other compliance approvals?
  • Does the sponsor need to approve it?

Reporting Control Review

  • What reports are required?
  • Who owns each deadline?
  • Was submission confirmed?
  • Is the report stored in the project file?

Audit Support Review

  • Does each cost have support?
  • Does documentation show project purpose?
  • Are approvals visible?
  • Can someone outside the project understand the record?

Personnel Transition Review

  • Is the person key personnel?
  • What duties must transfer?
  • Are records complete?
  • Does the sponsor need notification?

Closeout Review

  • Are final reports complete?
  • Are final costs reconciled?
  • Is subrecipient closeout complete?
  • Are records retained?

Marcus’s Year 3 pattern: confirm the record, resolve open items, document the basis, and close only when the award is ready.

Final Program Mindset

What Marcus’s work teaches across the full award

Start with the record

Post-award decisions should be grounded in award terms, approvals, budgets, reports, and documentation.

Connect finances to activity

Spending only makes sense when compared to what the project is actually doing.

Ask before acting

Marcus gathers information before approving, correcting, escalating, or stopping activity.

Document the why

A decision is stronger when the file explains the reason, evidence, requirement, and approval path.

Monitor patterns

Repeated small issues can reveal larger control, communication, training, or process gaps.

Close with confidence

A clean closeout depends on decisions made throughout the life of the award, not only at the end.

This is the shift from processing transactions to stewarding a sponsored project.
