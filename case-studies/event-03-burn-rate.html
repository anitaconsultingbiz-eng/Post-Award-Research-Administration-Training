Burn Rate Problem

Six months into the award, Marcus reviews the financial reports and notices that the project is spending much more slowly than expected.

Year 1 Event 03 Financial Monitoring

Story Scene

Research Finance notices a spending pattern

As part of routine award monitoring, David Patel from the Research Finance Office reviews the project’s financial activity. His role is to monitor project expenditures, financial records, reconciliations, and spending trends.

David notices that overall project spending appears lower than expected. The Personnel category is underspent, and participant incentive spending is also below projections. Because more than one budget category is affected, he contacts Marcus and requests a closer review.

Marcus receives David’s financial review email with the latest quarterly financial report attached. At first glance, the numbers appear positive. The project is well below budget. There are no overdrafts. No expenditures appear problematic.

However, Marcus knows that financial health cannot be measured by spending alone. He begins reviewing project activity alongside financial activity.

As Marcus compares project milestones with expenditures, a pattern begins to emerge: the project is not only spending less than expected. Several planned activities are occurring more slowly than anticipated.

Training Concept

What is burn rate?

Burn rate is the pace at which a project spends its approved budget over time. In post-award management, Marcus is not only checking whether the project has money left. He is checking whether spending matches the project timeline and the work that should be happening.

Healthy spending pattern

Spending generally follows the approved project plan, timeline, staffing, and activity schedule.

Low burn rate

Spending is slower than expected. This may signal delays in hiring, recruitment, purchasing, participant activity, or other project work.

High burn rate

Spending is faster than expected. This may signal overspending, early purchases, miscoded expenses, or future budget shortages.

Trigger Issue

The financial report shows lower-than-expected spending

The project appears financially healthy because spending is below the approved budget. But Marcus needs to understand why spending is low before deciding that the project is on track.

Quarterly Financial Report

Provides a high-level snapshot of project spending.

  • Total spending is below expectations
  • Multiple budget categories are underspent
  • Burn rate is lower than projected

Budget-to-Actual Report

Compares planned spending against actual spending.

  • Personnel variance remains significant
  • Participant incentive spending is low
  • Overall project spending is behind schedule

Financial Forecast

Projects future spending based on current trends.

  • Continued underspending could lead to a large remaining balance
  • Future carryforward or extension discussions may occur

Marcus’s Analysis

The issue is not just the number. It is the reason behind the number.

Burn Rate Analysis

Six-Month Spending Review

Below forecast

Marcus reviews the financial reports and sees that the project is spending below forecast. That pattern connects back to earlier project events.

Known cause: Hiring delay

Personnel spending remains below projections because the Research Coordinator position was not filled as originally planned.

Known cause: Delayed recruitment

Participant incentive spending is lower than anticipated because recruitment activities started later than planned.

The financial report does not exist separately from the story of the project. The numbers reflect what has happened operationally.

Marcus should investigate whether spending patterns match project activity and project expectations.

Administrator Thinking

What Marcus notices

David has identified the financial pattern: overall spending is lower than expected. Marcus now needs to interpret what that pattern means for the project. The project is not overspent, but that does not automatically mean the project is healthy.

What the numbers show

  • Overall spending is below expectations
  • Personnel spending remains low
  • Participant incentive spending is low
  • The project has a lower-than-expected burn rate

What Marcus needs to ask

  • Are planned activities happening on schedule?
  • Is recruitment behind?
  • Will delayed spending affect Year 1 milestones?
  • Could this create carryforward or extension issues later?

Financial monitoring is not just about whether spending is high or low. It is about whether spending patterns match project activity.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do first?

A project has spent significantly less than expected six months into the award period. What should a research administrator do first?

Communication

How Marcus should communicate the trend

From: Marcus Rivera

To: Dr. Aneka Brown

Subject: Spending Trend and Project Status Review

Hi Dr. Brown,

I reviewed the most recent quarterly financial report and noticed that project spending is currently below the expected pace.

The lower burn rate appears connected to the earlier Research Coordinator hiring delay and the later start of participant recruitment activities. At this stage, I do not see evidence of an expense error, but I recommend that we review the project timeline and upcoming activities to determine whether the spending pattern reflects delayed implementation.

This will help us determine whether continued monitoring is sufficient or whether we need to plan for future budget or timeline adjustments.

Best,
Marcus Rivera
Research Administrator

Key Takeaway

Burn rate tells a project story

Financial monitoring is not about determining whether spending is simply high or low. It is about determining whether spending patterns match project activity and project expectations.
