Year 1 Event 02 Hiring Delay

Story Scene

The project is moving, but a key role is still empty

After resolving the initial human subjects compliance issue, the project continues moving through Year 1. Dr. Brown and the team are preparing for recruitment and data collection activities.

But Marcus notices another issue. The project budget included a full-time Research Coordinator, and that person was expected to begin on September 1, 2027.

By December 2027, the position remains vacant. The delay creates salary savings, but it may also affect project operations.

Marcus’s question is not simply, “Is the project under budget?” His question is: “Why is the project under budget, and does that signal a risk to the work?”

Trigger Issue

A delayed hire creates a financial variance

A variance is the difference between what the project expected to spend and what it actually spent. In this case, the project expected to pay the Research Coordinator, but because the position is still vacant, those salary charges did not happen.

Planned position

The Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative includes funding for a full-time Research Coordinator.

  • Position is grant-funded
  • Position is 100% project-funded
  • Annual salary is $60,000
  • Expected start date: September 1, 2027

Current status

Recruitment has taken longer than expected. By December 2027, the position remains vacant.

  • Candidate has accepted the offer
  • Onboarding is still pending
  • Project activities may continue to be delayed

Financial effect

The delay has created lower-than-expected personnel spending.

  • Vacancy duration: 3 months
  • Monthly salary equivalent: $5,000
  • Current salary savings: $15,000

Marcus’s Analysis

Salary savings are not automatically good news

But Marcus does not stop at the number. He asks what caused the variance and whether it affects the project’s ability to complete Year 1 activities.

Administrator Thinking

What Marcus notices

A lower-than-expected personnel expense may look positive at first. The project is spending less than planned, and no payroll error appears to have occurred.

But Marcus knows that underspending can also signal a problem. If the project planned to use a Research Coordinator for recruitment, data collection, community outreach, and incentive tracking, then the vacancy may affect the work itself.

Financial question

Is the $15,000 variance accurate, documented, and tied to a clear cause?

Project question

Is the hiring delay affecting the timeline, workload, or ability to meet Year 1 goals?

Salary savings are not automatically good news. Marcus must understand why the savings exist and whether they create project risk.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do next?

Marcus identifies $15,000 in salary savings caused by the vacant Research Coordinator position. What should he do next?

Communication

How Marcus should communicate the issue

From: Marcus Rivera

To: Dr. Aneka Brown

Subject: Budget Impact Discussion

Hi Dr. Brown,

I reviewed the current personnel spending and noticed that the Research Coordinator position remains vacant. This has created approximately $15,000 in salary savings through November.

At this point, I do not see a payroll error or an immediate corrective action requirement. However, the variance should be documented because the position was expected to support recruitment, data collection, community outreach, and participant incentive tracking.

I recommend that we continue monitoring the vacancy and discuss whether the delay affects the Year 1 timeline or planned project activities.

Best,
Marcus Rivera
Research Administrator

Key Takeaway

Underspending can still be a risk

Salary savings should be investigated and documented. Lower-than-expected spending is not always a positive sign. It may indicate delayed project activity.
