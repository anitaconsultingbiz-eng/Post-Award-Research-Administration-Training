Event 13

Carryforward Surprise

Year 3 begins with a remaining balance. Dr. Brown sees unspent funds as an opportunity, but Marcus must determine whether the funds can automatically carry forward.

Year 3 Event 13 Carryforward Review

Story Background

A large remaining balance changes the conversation

As Year 3 begins, Marcus Rivera reviews the project’s financial position. The project has made strong progress, but spending is not exactly where the team expected it to be.

Several earlier delays affected the spending pattern: startup timing, hiring delays, slower outreach activity, and subrecipient documentation follow-up. Now those earlier issues show up as a remaining balance at the end of the budget period.

Dr. Brown wants to use the remaining funds to expand outreach activities, but Marcus knows that unspent funds are not always automatically available for future use.

The question is not simply whether money remains. The question is whether the award allows the balance to carry forward and whether the planned use fits the approved project.

Trigger Issue

Dr. Brown wants to use the remaining funds

From: Dr. Aneka Brown

To: Marcus Rivera

Subject: Remaining Balance for Year 3 Activities

Hi Marcus,

I reviewed the current project balance and noticed that we have more funds remaining than expected. Since recruitment and outreach are going well, I would like to use the balance to expand several Year 3 community activities.

Can we carry the remaining funds forward and use them for additional outreach, participant engagement, and community materials?

Thank you,
Dr. Aneka Brown

Training Concept

Remaining balance does not always mean available balance

A remaining balance shows that funds were not spent during a budget period. Carryforward is the ability to move those unobligated funds into a future period. Marcus must review the award terms before treating the balance as available.

Remaining Balance

Funds that were not spent or obligated by the end of a budget period.

Carryforward

Permission to use unobligated funds in a later budget period.

Automatic Carryforward

Some awards allow funds to carry forward without prior sponsor approval.

Restricted Carryforward

Some awards require sponsor approval before remaining funds can be used in a later period.

Planned Use

Even if funds can carry forward, the proposed use must still support the approved project.

Documentation

The file should show the balance, award terms, sponsor requirements, and planned use of funds.

Marcus’s Analysis

Questions before approving the plan

Is the balance accurate?

Marcus checks whether pending invoices, payroll, encumbrances, or subrecipient costs still need to post.

Is carryforward automatic?

Marcus reviews sponsor terms to determine whether the remaining balance can move forward without prior approval.

Is sponsor approval required?

If carryforward is restricted, Marcus must help prepare the request before the funds are used.

Does the planned use fit the project?

Expanded outreach must still support the approved objectives, not create a new project direction.

Does the plan affect future spending?

Marcus considers whether the remaining funds are needed for planned Year 3 work, closeout, or pending obligations.

What should be documented?

Marcus documents the balance review, carryforward authority, planned use, and any sponsor communication.

Decision Point

Can Dr. Brown use the remaining balance?

The project has more remaining funds than expected, and Dr. Brown wants to use them for expanded outreach activities. What should Marcus do first?

Resolution

Marcus confirms the carryforward requirements

Carryforward Review Notes

Prepared by Marcus Rivera

Issue Identified The project has a larger-than-expected remaining balance at the start of Year 3.
Records Reviewed Current financial report, unobligated balance details, Notice of Award, planned outreach activities, and carryforward rules.
Administrative Concern Remaining funds may not be automatically available for future use without confirming carryforward authority.
Recommended Action Confirm whether carryforward is automatic or restricted, document the planned use, and request sponsor approval if required before spending the balance.

The balance is not the decision. The award terms, planned use, and documentation determine whether the funds can move forward.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“If money is left, we can spend it.”

Remaining funds may be subject to carryforward rules, sponsor restrictions, or prior approval requirements.

“Carryforward is always automatic.”

Some awards allow automatic carryforward, while others require sponsor approval before funds can be used in a later period.

“A high balance is always good news.”

A large balance may reflect delays, unposted costs, missed obligations, or activity that did not happen as planned.
