Year 1 Event 04 Allowability Review

Story Scene

A good opportunity still needs review

After months of project startup work, Dr. Brown receives an invitation to present preliminary findings at the National Public Health Research Conference.

From Dr. Brown’s perspective, the opportunity makes sense. The conference relates to the project’s subject matter, would allow her to share early findings, and could increase visibility for the study.

However, the conference was not specifically identified in the original proposal budget. Before making travel arrangements, Dr. Brown contacts Marcus to ask whether the conference expenses can be charged to the award.

Marcus’s question is not simply, “Is there money available?” His question is: “Is this cost allowable, allocable, reasonable, and consistent with sponsor requirements?”

Training Concept

Funds available does not automatically mean cost approved

In post-award management, Marcus cannot approve a cost just because the project has money left. He has to review whether the cost fits the award purpose, supports the project, follows institutional policy, and meets sponsor requirements.

Allowable

The cost is permitted under federal rules, sponsor terms, and institutional policy.

Allocable

The cost benefits the project being charged and can be connected to the project’s purpose or activities.

Reasonable

The cost is necessary, appropriate, and not excessive for the project need.

Hi Marcus,

I received an invitation to present preliminary findings from the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative at the National Public Health Research Conference.

The conference seems directly related to the project and would allow us to share early results, connect with other researchers, and increase visibility for the study.

Best,
Dr. Aneka Brown

Marcus’s Cost Review Framework

Four questions Marcus needs to answer

Administrative Review

Allowability, allocability, reasonableness, and sponsor requirements

Travel review

1. Is it allowable?

Is conference travel permitted under the award terms, institutional travel policy, and applicable federal requirements?

2. Is it allocable?

Does the travel benefit this specific project and connect to the funded scope of work?

3. Is it reasonable?

Are the costs necessary, appropriate, and not excessive for the project purpose?

4. Is prior approval needed?

Do the sponsor terms, award budget, or institutional policies require additional review before expenses are incurred?

Marcus should document the review before advising Dr. Brown to proceed.

Administrator Thinking

What Marcus notices

Dr. Brown is doing the right thing by asking before making travel arrangements. Marcus can see why the conference may support the project, but he still needs to complete the cost review.

What supports the request?

  • The conference relates to the project subject matter
  • Dr. Brown is invited to present project findings
  • The conference occurs during the project period
  • The travel may increase visibility for the study

What still needs review?

  • The conference was not specifically planned in the proposal
  • The estimated costs must be reasonable
  • The travel must benefit the project being charged
  • Sponsor and institutional requirements must be checked

Research administrators evaluate expenses based on requirements, not only based on project benefit or available funds.

Communication

How Marcus should respond

From: Marcus Rivera

To: Dr. Aneka Brown

Subject: Conference Travel Review

Hi Dr. Brown,

Thank you for checking before making travel arrangements. I reviewed the conference information, the estimated costs, the remaining travel budget, and the applicable sponsor and institutional requirements.

Because the conference is related to the project and you have been invited to present project findings, the travel may be appropriate to charge to the award if the costs are reasonable, properly documented, and consistent with travel policy.

Before finalizing arrangements, please send the final cost estimate and conference agenda so we can complete the documentation for the project file.

Best,
Marcus Rivera
Research Administrator

Key Takeaway

A good project opportunity still needs cost review

Research administrators evaluate expenses based on allowability, allocability, reasonableness, and sponsor requirements — not simply whether funds are available.

Resources

References behind this scenario

These references show why Marcus cannot approve conference travel based only on interest, opportunity, or available funds. A post-award research administrator must connect the travel request to the project purpose, the approved budget, sponsor expectations, institutional travel rules, and federal cost principles.

2 CFR 200.403 — Factors Affecting Allowability

This federal cost principle explains that costs charged to a federal award must meet basic allowability standards, including being necessary, reasonable, allocable, consistently treated, documented, and compliant with applicable requirements.

Connection to Event 04: Marcus must determine whether the proposed conference travel can appropriately be charged to the grant.

2 CFR 200.404 — Reasonable Costs

This section helps frame whether a cost is sensible for the circumstances. Marcus has to consider whether the travel estimate is necessary, appropriate, and not excessive for the project purpose.

Connection to Event 04: the travel estimate must be reasonable for the project purpose.

2 CFR 200.405 — Allocable Costs

This federal cost principle explains that a cost should benefit the specific award being charged. The conference must have a clear connection to the funded project, not just Dr. Brown’s general professional development.

Connection to Event 04: the conference must support the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative.

Institutional Travel Policy

The university’s travel policy sets local requirements for travel approval, reimbursement, documentation, spending limits, receipts, and timing. Even project-related travel must still follow institutional procedures.

Connection to Event 04: project-related travel must follow university travel procedures before and after the trip.

Award Terms and Conditions

Award terms may include sponsor restrictions, prior approval requirements, travel limitations, or special conditions. Marcus needs to check whether the sponsor placed any limits on travel before the cost is approved.

Connection to Event 04: Marcus must confirm whether sponsor prior approval is needed.

Award Budget

The approved budget helps Marcus determine whether travel funds were planned, whether funds remain available, and whether approving the trip would affect other project activities.

Connection to Event 04: available funds matter, but they are not the first or only test.
