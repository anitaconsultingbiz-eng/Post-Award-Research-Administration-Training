Event 15

Reporting Deadline Missed

A required sponsor report was not submitted on time. Marcus must determine what happened, assess the risk, coordinate with the right people, document the response, and strengthen the internal controls that should have prevented the miss.

Year 3 Event 15 Reporting Controls

Story Background

A missed deadline creates a project-level concern

Year 3 is putting more pressure on the project team. Outreach activities are still active, CHAN’s work continues, and Dr. Brown is preparing for final project milestones.

During a routine review, Marcus notices that a required sponsor report was due the previous week. The report was not submitted, and no extension or delay notice appears in the project file.

Reporting is a shared responsibility. Dr. Brown may provide the technical progress content, David may provide financial reporting information, Jasmine may help gather project activity updates, and Marcus helps track the requirement, coordinate the response, and document the status in the award file.

At first, the issue may seem administrative. But sponsor reporting is part of award stewardship. Missed reports can affect sponsor trust, future funding, payment processing, and closeout readiness.

The issue is not just that a deadline was missed. Marcus must understand why it was missed, who needs to act, and what control failed.

Trigger Issue

Marcus finds the overdue report

From: Marcus Rivera

To: Dr. Aneka Brown

Cc: David Patel; Jasmine Lewis

Subject: Overdue Sponsor Report

Hi Dr. Brown,

During my Year 3 reporting review, I noticed that the sponsor progress report due last week does not appear to have been submitted.

I also do not see documentation showing that an extension was requested or that the sponsor was notified of a delay.

Could you confirm the status of the report and whether there are any barriers to submitting it as soon as possible? David, could you also confirm whether any financial reporting information is needed for this submission?

Thank you,
Marcus Rivera
Research Administrator

Training Concept

Reporting is a compliance responsibility

Reports are not just paperwork. Sponsor reports document project progress, financial activity, compliance status, and whether the project is meeting expectations. Missed reports can signal weak internal controls.

Progress Reporting

Communicates project activity, accomplishments, challenges, and next steps to the sponsor.

Financial Reporting

Summarizes spending, obligations, balances, and financial status for a specific reporting period.

Missed Deadline

Creates a compliance concern and may require sponsor communication or corrective action.

Internal Controls

Processes used to track deadlines, assign responsibility, confirm submission, and document completion.

Corrective Action

Steps taken to resolve the missed report and prevent the problem from recurring.

Closeout Readiness

Timely reporting helps prevent unresolved sponsor issues when the award approaches closeout.

What Marcus Reviews

The file should show both deadline tracking and submission status

Award Reporting Requirements

Identifies what reports are required, when they are due, and who is responsible for preparing or submitting them.

Reporting Calendar

Shows whether the due date was tracked and whether reminders were set before the deadline.

Draft Report Status

Helps Marcus determine whether the report was started, delayed, incomplete, or never assigned.

Sponsor Portal or Submission Record

Confirms whether the report was actually submitted and when.

Team Communications

Shows whether the team discussed the deadline, barriers, or reporting responsibilities.

Corrective Action Notes

Document what happened, how the report was submitted, and what process will prevent repeat missed deadlines.

Marcus’s Analysis

Questions before corrective action

Was the report prepared?

Marcus asks whether a draft exists, whether data are missing, or whether the report was never started.

Who owned the deadline?

Marcus checks whether responsibility was assigned to the PI, research administrator, finance, project staff, or another unit.

Was the sponsor notified?

If the report is late, Marcus determines whether communication with the sponsor is needed or already occurred.

What is the immediate fix?

The team must complete and submit the overdue report as soon as possible through the appropriate sponsor reporting process.

What control failed?

Marcus reviews whether deadline tracking, reminders, ownership, or submission confirmation broke down.

How do we prevent recurrence?

Marcus recommends a reporting calendar, assigned owners, advance reminders, and confirmation records.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do first?

Marcus discovers that a required sponsor report is overdue and no submission confirmation or extension request appears in the project file. What is the best response?

Resolution

Marcus coordinates the response and strengthens the control process

Marcus does not prepare every part of the report alone. He confirms the status, coordinates with Dr. Brown, David, and Jasmine, documents the late submission issue, and makes sure the project file shows how the report was completed and how the control process was improved.

Reporting Follow-Up Notes

Prepared by Marcus Rivera

Issue Identified A required sponsor progress report was not submitted by the due date, and no delay documentation was found in the project file.
Records Reviewed Award reporting requirements, reporting calendar, project communications, draft report status, and sponsor submission records.
Immediate Action Marcus coordinated with Dr. Brown, David, and Jasmine to confirm report status, gather needed information, submit the overdue report through the appropriate sponsor process, and document any sponsor communication.
Control Improvement A reporting tracker will assign each deadline to an owner, include advance reminders, and require submission confirmation in the project file.

Fixing the late report is only part of the response. Marcus also helps fix the process that allowed the deadline to be missed.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“Reports are just administrative paperwork.”

Reports communicate project progress, financial status, and sponsor accountability. They are part of compliance.

“A late report is only a problem if the sponsor complains.”

Waiting for the sponsor to notice is not a control process. Marcus should identify, document, and correct the issue.

“One missed deadline does not require process review.”

A missed deadline can reveal unclear ownership, weak reminders, or missing submission confirmation.

Year 3 Connection

Reporting controls matter more as closeout approaches

Near the end of an award, missed reports can create delays, additional sponsor scrutiny, and closeout problems.

Closeout pressure

Final reports often depend on accurate, timely interim reporting and complete project records.

Shared responsibility

Reporting may require input from the PI, finance, project staff, subrecipients, and research administration.

Control documentation

A tracker, reminder schedule, and submission confirmation record help show that the institution manages reporting responsibly.

Resources

References behind this scenario

These references show why reporting deadlines and internal controls are important parts of award management. A post-award research administrator should track reporting requirements, coordinate with the people responsible for report content or submission, retain confirmation records, and strengthen controls when a deadline is missed.

2 CFR 200.328 — Financial Reporting

This federal section relates to financial reporting requirements for federal awards, including the expectation that required financial information is reported according to sponsor and federal requirements.

Connection to Event 15: financial reporting obligations must be tracked, completed on time, and supported by submission records.

2 CFR 200.329 — Monitoring and Reporting Program Performance

This federal section relates to performance reporting and the sponsor’s need to understand project progress, challenges, accomplishments, and whether the project is meeting approved objectives.

Connection to Event 15: progress reports communicate whether the project is meeting approved objectives and must be submitted as required.

2 CFR 200.303 — Internal Controls

This federal requirement frames the institution’s responsibility to maintain controls that help prevent, detect, and correct problems in federal award activity.

Connection to Event 15: missed deadlines point to the need for stronger tracking, ownership, reminder, and confirmation controls.

Institutional Reporting Calendar

A reporting calendar helps assign ownership, set reminders, track due dates, and retain evidence that reports were submitted through the correct process.

Connection to Event 15: a reporting calendar is the main control Marcus strengthens after the missed deadline.
