Event 07

Participant Incentive Question

The project is distributing $25 gift cards to survey participants. Marcus must determine whether the incentive records are complete, reconciled, and audit-ready.

Year 1 Event 07 Internal Controls

Story Scene

The project is finally reaching participants

After the earlier delays, the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative is now actively recruiting participants and collecting survey responses.

Jasmine is coordinating much of the day-to-day participant activity. As participants complete surveys, the project provides $25 gift cards as approved participant incentives.

During a routine review, Marcus compares participant activity to the incentive records. The amounts are not large, but the tracking matters. The participant counts do not perfectly match the incentive documentation.

Marcus’s question is not just, “Were gift cards purchased?” His question is: “Can the project support every incentive that was distributed?”

Trigger Issue

Participant counts and incentive records do not fully match

Participant incentives require strong documentation because they involve both project spending and participant activity. Marcus needs to confirm that each distributed gift card is supported by the right records.

Approved incentive

The project distributes $25 gift cards to participants who complete the survey.

Documentation concern

Participant counts do not perfectly match the incentive distribution records.

Administrative question

Marcus must determine whether adequate documentation exists to support the incentive distributions.

Marcus’s Reconciliation Work

The records show a small mismatch that still needs review

Marcus does not treat the variance as proof of wrongdoing. He treats it as a control issue that needs to be reconciled. His goal is to determine whether the incentives were distributed appropriately, whether the project file can support them, and whether the mismatch is a documentation problem or a true distribution issue.

1. He compares the totals

Marcus starts by comparing the number of completed participant records against the number of gift cards listed in the incentive log.

This confirms that there is a small mismatch, but it does not explain why the mismatch occurred.

2. He checks the support

Next, Marcus reviews the acknowledgment forms, distribution records, participant tracking sheet, and scanned copies saved in the project folder.

This step helps him determine whether the issue is a missing gift card, a missing form, or a filing/documentation problem.

3. He finds the source

Marcus finds that three acknowledgment forms were completed but saved in the wrong project folders. Once the forms are moved into the correct file, the participant records and incentive log reconcile.

The issue was not an improper incentive payment. It was a filing error that still needed to be corrected and documented.

Why Marcus still documents it

Even though the problem turns out to be a filing error, Marcus still documents the review. The project file should show what discrepancy was identified, what records were checked, what caused the mismatch, and how the file was corrected.

That documentation matters because participant incentives involve cash-equivalent items, eligibility records, distribution logs, and internal controls.

Training Concept

Why participant incentives need strong controls

Gift cards may seem simple, but they are easy to lose track of. A strong incentive process connects participant activity, gift card inventory, distribution records, and reconciliation.

Documentation

Records should show who received an incentive, when it was issued, and why the person was eligible.

Inventory control

The project should track gift cards received, stored, distributed, voided, or remaining.

Reconciliation

Participant counts, survey completion records, and gift card logs should be compared regularly.

Risk

Missing or inconsistent records can create audit concerns, even when the dollar amounts are small.

Marcus’s Incentive Review

Marcus looks for the connection between participants, gift cards, and records

Incentive Reconciliation

Can every gift card be supported?

Records review

Marcus does not evaluate incentives only by looking at the total amount spent. He looks for whether the records connect clearly from participant activity to incentive distribution.

1. Confirm eligibility

Did the participant complete the activity required to receive the incentive?

2. Match distribution records

Does the distribution log show when the gift card was issued and by whom?

3. Review inventory

Does the gift card inventory report show the card leaving inventory?

4. Reconcile totals

Do survey completions, participant counts, and incentive totals match?

5. Investigate exceptions

Are there missing signatures, duplicate entries, unclear participant identifiers, or unmatched cards?

6. Document the review

Marcus records what matched, what did not match, and what follow-up is needed.

Strong incentive controls protect the participant, the project team, and the institution.

Resolution

The incentives were valid, but the control process needs improvement

Marcus’s review confirms that all incentives were distributed appropriately. The missing documentation was not missing permanently; three participant acknowledgment forms had been filed in the wrong project folders.

The financial impact is minor because there was no loss of funds and the gift card numbers, participant signatures, and distribution dates were confirmed.

The lesson is still important: even when the money is accounted for, weak filing and reconciliation practices can create audit risk.

Key Takeaway

Incentives require documentation and controls

Participant incentives require strong documentation and internal controls. Marcus must be able to connect participant activity, incentive distribution, gift card inventory, and reconciliation records before the project can be considered audit-ready.

Resources

References behind this scenario

These references show why participant incentives require more than a simple spending review. A post-award research administrator must connect incentive costs to participant eligibility, approved study procedures, distribution records, inventory controls, reconciliation files, and institutional payment rules.

2 CFR 200.303 — Internal Controls

This federal requirement frames the institution’s responsibility to maintain processes that prevent, detect, and correct problems with federal award activity.

Connection to Event 07: gift card tracking requires controls over inventory, distribution, documentation, and reconciliation.

2 CFR 200.403 — Factors Affecting Allowability

This federal cost principle explains that costs charged to an award must be necessary, reasonable, allocable, documented, consistently treated, and compliant with applicable requirements.

Connection to Event 07: each incentive should be connected to an eligible participant activity and supported by documentation.

2 CFR 200.405 — Allocable Costs

This federal cost principle explains that a cost must benefit the specific award being charged. Participant incentives should relate to this project’s approved recruitment and data collection activities.

Connection to Event 07: incentives must relate to participants in this project, not another study or department activity.

Institutional Participant Payment Policy

The institution’s participant payment policy sets local requirements for gift cards, participant payments, tracking logs, storage, approvals, distribution, and reconciliation.

Connection to Event 07: universities often require specific procedures for gift card logs, custody, and reconciliation.

IRB-Approved Protocol and Consent Materials

The approved protocol and consent materials show what participants were told about incentives, who is eligible, when payment occurs, and what amount was approved.

Connection to Event 07: the incentive amount and eligibility should align with the approved participant process.

Project Records and Reconciliation Files

Distribution logs, inventory records, survey completion lists, acknowledgment forms, and exception notes create the record trail that supports the incentive review.

Connection to Event 07: Marcus needs a clear trail from participant activity to gift card distribution and final reconciliation.
