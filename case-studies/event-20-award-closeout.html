Year 3 Event 20 Award Closeout

Story Background

The award is ready to close only when the record is complete

After three years, the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative is reaching its official end. Dr. Brown has completed the major project activities, CHAN has finished its community outreach work, and the team is preparing final reports.

But closeout is not simply the moment when project work stops. Closeout is the process of confirming that the award record is complete, final costs are accurate, reports are submitted, and remaining administrative issues are resolved.

Marcus now helps bring together the pieces that have appeared throughout the full three-year story: compliance approvals, payroll, effort, subrecipient monitoring, cost transfers, reporting, audit documentation, reconciliation, and final financial review.

The question is not “Is the project over?” The question is “Can the institution support how the project was managed from start to finish?”

David Patel

Training Concept

Closeout is the final accountability process

Award closeout confirms that the project has completed required work, submitted required reports, resolved final costs, and retained documentation. It is the final test of whether the award was managed responsibly.

Final Reports

Required sponsor reports that may include financial, performance, invention, property, or other final submissions.

Final Financial Review

Confirms that final costs are accurate, allowable, allocable, reconciled, and supported.

Subrecipient Closeout

Confirms that CHAN’s final invoice, reports, deliverables, and required documentation have been received and reviewed.

Documentation Retention

Ensures the institution retains records that support project activity, costs, decisions, and reports.

Closeout Process

The institutional workflow used to complete final review, route approvals, submit reports, and close the account.

Late Issues

Unresolved costs, missing reports, open obligations, or incomplete subrecipient records can delay closeout.

What Marcus Reviews

The closeout file must tell the full project story

Final Financial Report

Shows the final amount spent, remaining balance, and final financial position of the award.

Final Progress Report

Describes completed project work, outcomes, challenges, and sponsor-required performance information.

Closeout Checklist

Tracks required reports, approvals, financial cleanup, and institutional closeout steps.

CHAN Closeout Package

Includes CHAN’s final invoice, final progress documentation, deliverables, and monitoring records.

Final Reconciliation Records

Support the final financial report and show that outstanding obligations were resolved.

Project File

Retains documentation for major decisions, approvals, transactions, reports, and sponsor communications.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“Closeout begins after the project is over.”

Strong closeout begins before the end date through reconciliation, documentation review, and report planning.

“Closeout is only final financial reporting.”

Closeout includes financial, performance, subrecipient, documentation, and institutional review steps.

“Once the final report is submitted, records no longer matter.”

Records must be retained and may be reviewed after closeout.

Three-Year Story Connection

Closeout brings the full project story together

The final closeout record reflects everything that happened across the three-year award.

Year 1: Startup and controls

IRB approval, hiring delays, budget review, allowability, documentation, and incentive controls established the foundation.

Year 2: Active management

Payroll, effort, subrecipient determination, monitoring, and cost transfers showed how the active project was managed.

Year 3: Closeout readiness

Carryforward, scope, reporting, audit readiness, personnel transition, extension, reconciliation, and closeout brought the award to completion.

Every earlier decision becomes part of the closeout record.

2 CFR 200.344 — Closeout

This federal section addresses award closeout requirements, including the submission of required financial, performance, and other reports after the period of performance ends.

Connection to Event 20: closeout requires final reports, resolution of award obligations, and completion of required sponsor and institutional steps.

2 CFR 200.328 — Financial Reporting

This federal section relates to financial reporting requirements for federal awards, including reporting required financial information according to sponsor and federal requirements.

Connection to Event 20: the final financial report must reflect a reconciled and supportable financial record.

2 CFR 200.334 — Retention Requirements for Records

This federal requirement addresses how long award records must be retained and available for review after the relevant period, subject to applicable award and institutional requirements.

Connection to Event 20: project records remain important after final reports are submitted because sponsors or auditors may review the file later.
