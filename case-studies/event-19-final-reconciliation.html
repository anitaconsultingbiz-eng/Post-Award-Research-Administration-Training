Year 3 Event 19 Final Reconciliation

Story Background

The project is nearly finished, but the finances are not clean yet

The Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative is nearing the end of its project period. Most programmatic work has been completed, the team is preparing final reports, and Dr. Brown is focused on wrapping up the scientific work.

Marcus now turns his attention to the financial record. Some costs have posted, but other activity may still be pending. CHAN may have a final invoice. Payroll may need a final review. Purchase orders may still be open. Participant incentive records may need final reconciliation.

Final reconciliation is the process of comparing the project’s financial activity against the project record before closeout.

The question is not simply “How much money is left?” The question is whether all project obligations have been identified, reviewed, documented, and resolved.

Trigger Issue

David asks Marcus to review closeout financial items

From: David Patel, Research Finance

To: Marcus Rivera

Cc: Dr. Aneka Brown

Subject: Final Reconciliation Review

Hi Marcus,

As we approach the end of the Community Access award, I am beginning the final financial reconciliation review.

Before we prepare the final financial report, we need to confirm whether there are any outstanding obligations, pending invoices, final payroll adjustments, open purchase orders, or unresolved cost corrections.

Could you work with Dr. Brown, Jasmine’s replacement, and CHAN to confirm the status of remaining project activity and outstanding financial items?

Thank you,
David Patel
Research Finance

Training Concept

Final reconciliation connects project activity to the financial record

Final reconciliation is a financial cleanup process before closeout. Marcus compares the financial report, project records, pending obligations, and remaining deliverables to make sure the award file is complete.

Outstanding Obligations

Costs the project has committed to pay but that may not have fully posted yet.

Pending Invoices

Vendor or subrecipient invoices that still need to be received, reviewed, approved, or posted.

Open Encumbrances

Purchase orders or commitments that remain open in the financial system.

Final Payroll Review

Confirms that salary charges are complete, accurate, and aligned with project work.

Financial Cleanup

Resolves errors, removes invalid charges, clears open items, and confirms that the account is ready for final reporting.

Closeout Risk

If reconciliation is incomplete, final reports may be inaccurate or costs may be questioned later.

What Marcus Reviews

The final review requires multiple records

Final Financial Report Draft

Shows expenses, obligations, remaining balance, and categories that need final review.

Open Obligation List

Identifies purchase orders, unpaid invoices, pending subrecipient costs, and other commitments.

CHAN Final Invoice Status

Confirms whether CHAN has submitted its final invoice, deliverables, and required closeout documents.

Payroll and Effort Records

Confirm that final salary charges and effort records align with approved project work.

Participant Incentive Reconciliation

Verifies that gift card purchases, distribution logs, and remaining inventory are reconciled.

Cost Correction Log

Confirms that transfers, corrections, and adjustments have been resolved and documented.

Marcus’s Analysis

Questions before final financial reporting

Are all project costs posted?

Marcus checks whether final invoices, payroll, subrecipient costs, and approved purchases have posted.

Are any obligations still open?

Open obligations must be confirmed, closed, or resolved before the final financial report is submitted.

Are any costs incorrect?

Marcus reviews whether unallowable costs, miscoded expenses, or unresolved transfers remain.

Does spending match project activity?

Costs should align with completed outreach, participant activity, CHAN work, payroll, and approved project needs.

Are supporting records complete?

Documentation should support final expenses, obligations, corrections, and remaining balances.

Can the final report be submitted confidently?

The final financial report should not be submitted until major pending items are resolved or properly accounted for.

Final reconciliation is the last chance to make sure the financial record matches the work that actually happened.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“The current balance is the final answer.”

The current balance may change if invoices, payroll, obligations, or corrections have not fully posted.

“Closeout is only a finance task.”

Closeout depends on input from the PI, project staff, subrecipients, research administration, and finance.

“Small unresolved items can wait.”

Small unresolved items can delay final reporting, create audit questions, or require corrections after closeout.
