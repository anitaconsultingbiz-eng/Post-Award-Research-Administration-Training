Event 08

Payroll Distribution Error

Year 2 begins with active award management. The project is moving, expenses are increasing, and Marcus must now monitor whether payroll charges accurately reflect the work being performed.

Year 2 Event 08 Payroll Monitoring

Story Background

The project is no longer in startup mode

The Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative is now fully operational. Participant recruitment is active, research activities are underway, and the project team is working across multiple parts of the award.

Personnel costs are now the largest category of project expenditures. That means payroll monitoring becomes one of the most important parts of Marcus Rivera’s post-award review.

During a routine financial review, David Patel notices something unusual. A portion of Jasmine Lewis’s salary may have been charged incorrectly.

Marcus must investigate before making a correction. The goal is not just to move money. The goal is to understand what happened, document it, and correct it appropriately.

Trigger Issue

David notices a payroll charge that may not belong

From: David Patel, Research Finance

To: Marcus Rivera, Research Administrator

Subject: Payroll Distribution Review

Hi Marcus,

During my monthly payroll review for the Community Access to Preventive Healthcare Initiative, I noticed that a portion of Jasmine Lewis’s salary appears to have been charged to the wrong funding source.

The payroll distribution report shows salary charged to the project during a period when her appointment record appears to show a different allocation.

Could you review the payroll distribution, salary allocation record, and project activity to determine whether a correction is needed?

Thank you,
David Patel
Research Finance

What Marcus Reviews

Payroll monitoring is about alignment

Marcus does not assume the payroll charge is wrong just because the report looks unusual. He compares payroll activity, salary allocation records, project work, and supporting documentation.

Payroll Distribution Report

Shows the actual salary charges posted through payroll processing.

Salary Allocation Record

Shows how Jasmine’s salary was supposed to be distributed across funding sources.

Project Activity Records

Help confirm whether Jasmine’s work during the period directly benefited the project.

Appointment or Assignment Details

Clarify Jasmine’s approved role, dates, level of effort, and expected project responsibilities.

Correction Documentation

Explains why a payroll correction is needed and identifies the correct funding source.

Approvals

Ensure the correction is reviewed and accepted by the appropriate administrative and financial offices.

A payroll variance does not automatically mean the charge is unallowable. It means Marcus needs to investigate whether the charge matches the work performed and the approved allocation.

Marcus’s Investigation

Questions before correction

Was the employee working on the project?

Marcus needs to confirm that Jasmine performed work that directly benefited the award during the payroll period.

Was the salary allocation updated?

Marcus checks whether the planned distribution changed or whether payroll posted differently from the approved allocation.

Is a correction needed?

If the payroll charge was posted to the wrong funding source, the correction should move the salary to the appropriate account.

What documentation supports the correction?

The file should explain the error, the correct allocation, the period affected, and the reason for the change.

Who needs to approve it?

Payroll corrections usually require review by the department, research administration, finance, or another institutional unit.

How can this be prevented?

Marcus considers whether monthly payroll reviews or labor distribution checks would catch similar errors earlier.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do next?

Marcus sees that the payroll distribution report does not match the salary allocation record. What is the best next step?

Resolution

Marcus documents the correction

Payroll Correction Review Notes

Prepared by Marcus Rivera

Issue Identified A portion of Jasmine Lewis’s salary was charged to the project at a higher percentage than supported by the approved salary allocation.
Records Reviewed Payroll distribution report, salary allocation record, assignment details, and project activity information.
Administrative Determination A payroll correction is required to align salary charges with the appropriate funding source.
Documentation Needed Explanation of the error, affected payroll period, correct funding source, supporting reports, and required approvals.

The correction is not just an accounting entry. It is a documented explanation of why the original charge was wrong and why the new charge is appropriate.

Internal Controls

How could this error be caught earlier?

Monthly Payroll Reviews

Compare payroll charges against approved project staffing and salary allocations each month.

PI or Project Team Review

Confirm that salary charges reflect actual work performed on the project.

Labor Distribution Verification

Review how salaries are distributed across grants, departments, and other funding sources.

Financial Monitoring Reports

Use routine reports to identify unusual payroll patterns or unexpected variances.

Clear Correction Workflow

Require corrections to include an explanation, support, timing, and approval.

Shared Responsibility

Payroll offices, departments, PIs, finance, and research administrators all help monitor payroll accuracy.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“Small payroll errors are not important.”

Even small payroll errors can create reporting inaccuracies, audit findings, and questions about award management.

“Corrections only matter when the amount is large.”

Corrections should be based on accuracy and support, not dollar amount alone.

“Payroll is only the payroll office’s responsibility.”

Payroll offices process transactions, but research administrators, departments, and principal investigators share responsibility for monitoring payroll activity.

Resources

References behind this scenario

These references show why Marcus reviews payroll distribution as both a financial issue and a documentation issue. A post-award research administrator must confirm that salary charges align with approved allocations, actual project work, institutional correction procedures, and federal compensation requirements.

2 CFR 200.430 — Compensation: Personal Services

This federal cost principle explains how salary, wages, effort, and compensation charged to a federal award must be supported and tied to work performed for the project.

Connection to Event 08: Jasmine’s salary charges must be supported and must align with the work performed for the award.

2 CFR 200.303 — Internal Controls

This federal requirement frames the institution’s responsibility to monitor federal awards, identify errors early, and maintain processes that support accurate payroll and financial reporting.

Connection to Event 08: monthly payroll review is an internal control that helps Marcus detect the distribution error.

Institutional Payroll Correction Policy

The institution’s payroll correction policy explains what approvals, justification, timing, and documentation are required before salary charges can be corrected or moved.

Connection to Event 08: Marcus must follow the correction process rather than simply moving the cost without support.

Salary Allocation Records

Salary allocation records show how an employee’s pay was expected to be distributed across projects, departments, or funding sources. Marcus compares those records against actual payroll charges.

Connection to Event 08: the variance begins with a mismatch between planned allocation and actual payroll distribution.
