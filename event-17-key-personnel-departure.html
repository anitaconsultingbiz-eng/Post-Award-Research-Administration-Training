Event 17

Key Personnel Departure

A key member of the project team is leaving. Marcus must help the team protect project continuity, review sponsor notification requirements, and document the transition plan.

Year 3 Event 17 Continuity Planning

Story Background

A staffing change creates a project continuity risk

As the project moves deeper into Year 3, the team is focused on final outreach activities, reporting, data cleanup, and preparing for closeout.

Then Dr. Brown informs Marcus that Jasmine Lewis will be leaving the university for a new opportunity. Jasmine has been central to day-to-day operations, participant tracking, incentive records, outreach coordination, and communication with CHAN.

The departure is not a problem by itself. People change roles and projects adapt. But because Jasmine supports important operational and compliance records, Marcus must help the team plan the transition carefully.

The question is not only who will replace Jasmine. The question is whether the project can continue responsibly and whether the sponsor must be notified.

Trigger Issue

Dr. Brown tells Marcus that Jasmine is leaving

From: Dr. Aneka Brown

To: Marcus Rivera

Subject: Jasmine Lewis Transition

Hi Marcus,

I wanted to let you know that Jasmine Lewis has accepted a new position and will be leaving the university next month.

She has been managing several important project activities, including participant tracking, incentive records, outreach coordination, and communication with CHAN.

We are beginning to think through a transition plan. Can you help identify what administrative items we need to review before she leaves?

Thank you,
Dr. Aneka Brown

Training Concept

Personnel changes can affect continuity, compliance, and reporting

A departure can create gaps in project knowledge, documentation, pending tasks, system access, and sponsor commitments. Marcus must determine whether the person is key personnel and whether the change affects approved project plans.

Project Continuity

The ability of the project to continue work, maintain records, and meet obligations during a staffing change.

Key Personnel

Individuals identified as critical to the project by the sponsor, proposal, award, or institution.

Sponsor Notification

Some personnel changes require sponsor notification or approval before the change is implemented.

Transition Plan

A documented plan for transferring responsibilities, records, access, and unfinished work.

Access Review

Ensures departing staff no longer have access they should not retain and replacement staff receive appropriate access.

Documentation Transfer

Ensures project files, logs, communications, and tracking tools are complete and available to the remaining team.

What Marcus Reviews

The role matters as much as the departure

Notice of Award

Identifies whether Jasmine is named as key personnel or whether sponsor approval is needed for personnel changes.

Original Proposal Staffing Plan

Shows Jasmine’s planned role, responsibilities, and how her work supports the approved project.

Current Responsibility List

Identifies tasks Jasmine currently owns, including participant records, incentives, outreach coordination, and CHAN communication.

Project Records

Helps confirm whether files, logs, trackers, and documentation are complete before Jasmine leaves.

Replacement or Coverage Plan

Explains who will cover Jasmine’s responsibilities and how the project will avoid disruption.

Sponsor Communication Requirements

Clarify whether the sponsor must be notified of the departure or replacement plan.

Marcus’s Analysis

Questions before the transition is finalized

Is Jasmine key personnel?

Marcus checks the proposal, Notice of Award, and institutional records to determine whether the sponsor considers her role critical.

Does the departure affect project scope or timeline?

Marcus evaluates whether the project can still complete planned activities on schedule.

Is sponsor notification required?

If the departure affects key personnel, project leadership, or approved commitments, sponsor communication may be required.

Who will assume her responsibilities?

The transition plan should assign each task to a new owner with dates and expectations.

Are records complete?

Marcus ensures participant logs, incentive records, CHAN files, and outreach documentation are current.

What access should change?

Departing staff access should be removed when appropriate, and replacement staff should receive needed project access.

Decision Point

What should Marcus do first?

Jasmine is leaving the project. She manages participant tracking, incentive records, outreach coordination, and CHAN communication. What is Marcus’s best next step?

Resolution

Marcus helps the team document the transition

Personnel Transition Review Notes

Prepared by Marcus Rivera

Issue Identified Jasmine Lewis is leaving the university and currently supports several operational and compliance-sensitive project responsibilities.
Records Reviewed Notice of Award, proposal staffing plan, current responsibility list, participant records, incentive logs, CHAN communication files, and access permissions.
Administrative Concern The project must maintain continuity, preserve records, assign responsibilities, and determine whether sponsor notification is required.
Recommended Action Complete a documented transition plan, reconcile open records, assign new owners, update system access, and communicate with the sponsor if required by award terms.

Personnel changes do not have to become compliance problems. They become risky when responsibilities, records, and sponsor requirements are not reviewed before the transition occurs.

Common Misconceptions

What learners should not assume

“Only the PI can be key personnel.”

Other individuals may be identified as key personnel or may hold responsibilities that are critical to project continuity.

“Sponsor notification is only needed for scientific changes.”

Personnel changes can affect project leadership, effort, timeline, deliverables, and sponsor expectations.

“A transition plan is just an HR issue.”

In sponsored projects, transitions affect documentation, access, reporting, compliance, subrecipient communication, and closeout readiness.

Year 3 Connection

Personnel changes can affect closeout readiness

Closeout depends on complete records, clear responsibility, and continuity. A staff departure late in the project can create problems if knowledge leaves with the person.

Records must stay with the project

Logs, trackers, correspondence, and supporting documents should be stored in the project file, not only with one individual.

Responsibilities must be reassigned

Each task should have a new owner so reporting, reconciliation, and closeout work do not stall.

Sponsor expectations must be checked

If the role is key to the award, sponsor notification or approval may be needed before the change is finalized.

Resources

References behind this scenario

These references show why personnel changes require more than a staffing conversation. A post-award research administrator should review the award terms, confirm whether the departing person is named as key personnel, assess the impact on project continuity, coordinate with the appropriate institutional offices, and document the transition plan before the change creates gaps in records, access, reporting, or sponsor commitments.

Notice of Award

The Notice of Award identifies sponsor-specific terms, key personnel expectations, effort commitments, prior approval requirements, and any special conditions that may apply when project staffing changes.

Connection to Event 17: Marcus checks whether Jasmine is named in the award record or whether her departure affects sponsor expectations.

2 CFR 200.308 — Revision of Budget and Program Plans

This section matters when a change affects approved project plans, key personnel, committed effort, project direction, or other elements that may require sponsor notification or prior approval.

Connection to Event 17: Jasmine’s departure may require review if her role is treated as key personnel or if the change affects approved project commitments.

Project Continuity Plan

A continuity plan assigns responsibilities, transfers knowledge, identifies pending work, confirms documentation ownership, and helps the project continue without losing operational or compliance support.

Connection to Event 17: the transition plan protects participant tracking, incentive reconciliation, CHAN communication, and project documentation.

Institutional Access and Records Procedures

Institutional procedures guide how system access is removed or added, how files are retained, how project records are transferred, and how replacement staff receive the access needed to continue project work.

Connection to Event 17: Marcus coordinates with the appropriate teams so records remain available and access changes are handled correctly.
